The permanence of the continents and ocean basins is a settled principle of
geology. The continents stand fixed upon the Earth's crust; they do not and
cannot wander across its surface, for no known force is remotely adequate to
propel such colossal masses horizontally through the rigid rock beneath them.
Where the same fossil species are found on lands now parted by ocean, the proper
explanation is a former land bridge, an isthmus since foundered beneath the
waves. The notion of drifting continents is a fanciful speculation, unsupported
by any sound mechanism, and it need not detain serious men of science.
