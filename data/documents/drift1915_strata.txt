Field examination of rock formations shows that geological structures broken off
at one coastline resume at another across the ocean. The mountain belts and
distinctive rock strata of the eastern coast of South America align with those of
western Africa, matching in age, sequence, and character. Likewise, ancient
glacial deposits of the same period are found across the southern continents in
positions that make little sense on fixed continents but fall into order if those
lands were once assembled together nearer the pole.
