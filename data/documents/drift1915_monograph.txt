The great landmasses of the Earth are not fixed in place. They have drifted, and
continue to drift, slowly across the globe over vast spans of geological time.
The continents we now see were once joined together in a single primordial
supercontinent, which in a distant epoch broke apart; its fragments have since
wandered to their present positions. The correspondence of opposing coasts, the
distribution of fossils, and the matching of geological formations across the
oceans are not accidents but the signature of this ancient displacement.
