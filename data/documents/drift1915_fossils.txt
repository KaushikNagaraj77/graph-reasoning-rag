Palaeontological collections from several southern continents contain the same
distinctive fossil forms. The seed-fern Glossopteris is found in the rocks of
South America, Africa, India, and Australia alike, and remains of the small
freshwater reptile Mesosaurus occur on both sides of the South Atlantic. That
such land-bound and freshwater species should appear on landmasses now divided
by broad oceans is difficult to explain unless those landmasses were formerly
contiguous.
